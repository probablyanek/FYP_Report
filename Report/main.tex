%% ABSTRACT

\addtotoc{Abstract}
\abstract{
\addtocontents{toc}{\vspace{1em}}

This work presents a dual-tier asynchronous sensor fusion framework for Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC) nodes, combining 24~GHz FMCW radar with IEEE~802.11mc Fine Time Measurement (FTM) on a low-cost ESP32-S3 dual-core platform. Single-modality ISAC systems suffer from complementary failure modes: millimetre-wave radar provides sub-foot range resolution and micro-Doppler signatures but is identity-blind and fails under line-of-sight occlusion, while Wi-Fi FTM offers MAC-address-linked identity and wall-penetrating coverage but exhibits 1.5--4.8~m spatial variability and updates at only 1--2~Hz. The resulting synchronisation gap causes standard Kalman filters to lock onto stale Wi-Fi readings, producing trajectory staircase artefacts during target manoeuvres.

Two fusion algorithms are developed and compared. Dynamic Covariance Intersection (DCI) projects the one-dimensional Wi-Fi range onto the radar's angular vector, applies first-order-hold velocity extrapolation, and inflates stale-sensor covariance via real-time Age-of-Information penalties, achieving a 45\% reduction in mean squared error during occlusion. An Edge MLP (11.6~KB, 2{,}474 float32 parameters) ingests a $5\times5$ sliding window of forward-filled features with binary freshness flags, completes inference in 350~$\mu$s per window entirely within internal SRAM, and delivers a test-set range MAE of 0.118~m with a P95 Euclidean error of 0.555~m. Strict dual-core isolation---Wi-Fi on Core~0, UART parsing and inference on Core~1---eliminates serial-buffer corruption. Validation employs a MATLAB/Simulink digital twin and a Python test suite covering field-of-view escape, pillar occlusion, tangential walk, and statue-mode scenarios.

}
